PROFILE

Dr. Fr Joe Kokkandathil ( Jomon K J) is a Catholic Salesian priest and a psychologist. He is a Ph.D from the Central University of Karnataka, India, in Positive Psychology. He holds Masters Degree in English (MA), Psychology (MSc) and Management (MBA), Has 7 years of teaching experiences in Various schools and 17 years of experience as principal and assistant professor at various colleges. Right now he is the Principal of of the Don Bosco Degree College, Chitradurga, Bangalore, Karnataka, India. He is a member of the Academic council of the University of Davengere. He is a certified trainer in Mental Health from NIMHANS India. He is an international trainer in emotional Hygiene. Along with research he involves himself in training of college students, teachers, faculty members and religious groups and corporates in and outside the Country. He has been training various groups of people primarily young people in Emotional hygiene in Europe, Africa, pacific Countries, Gulf Countries and in India. He has trained about 14000 plus teachers and faculty members Globally. Besides being a trainer, he has humanitarian outreach programmes especially as human rights activist. As a researcher, has number of publications both at the national and international levels. Research and training are focussed primarily on emotional issues of college going youth. And as a psychologist he has regular clinical practice where clients with various psychological- interpersonal problems, seek psychological assessments and psychotherapy. The therapeutic works eclectic though is mainly based on CBT, Mindfulness, hypnotic healing, EFT, EMDR, Solution focused approaches and Access Bar Consiouness. Besides being a certified mindfulness practitioner, he is also a certified practitioner of Solution focussed brief therapy (SFBT), EFT, Ericssonian Hypnotherapy and NLP and Access Bar Facilitator. Apart from clinical practice, he is also involved in teaching, training, counselling, conducting workshops, and independent research. Research works are in affiliation with Central University of Karnataka. Currently he is the National president of the international human rights association, India.
